What happened: I dropped my 2026 Tesla Model X with the valet at VanCity Parking (359 9th Ave, New York, NY) earlier in the evening. When the valet brought the car back up for pickup, he attempted to move it from its position behind another customer's vehicle (a black Mercedes-Benz SUV). Instead of putting the car in Reverse to back out, the valet placed it in Drive and slammed on the accelerator, launching the Tesla forward and rear-ending the Mercedes directly in front of it. The collision is captured on the Tesla's built-in front dashcam, which was actively recording at the time. The impact caused damage to the hood, scratches to the front nose, and broke the front Tesla emblem free of its mounting clip.

Liability

  1. At the time of the incident, my 2026 Tesla Model X was in the sole care, custody, and control of VanCity Parking and its employee acting within the scope of his employment.
  2. The vehicle ahead (a black Mercedes-Benz SUV) was stationary and parked. It was not in motion, and the Tesla front camera shows the gap between the two vehicles closing solely as a result of the Tesla moving forward.
  3. The valet attendant selected Drive instead of Reverse and pressed the accelerator, propelling the Tesla forward into the parked Mercedes.
  4. The collision and the seconds leading up to it are captured continuously and clearly on the Tesla's own factory dashcam, file 2026-05-11_21-40-50-front.mp4, preserved and reproduced above.
  5. The resulting damage to the front of the Tesla — hood deformation, paint scratches, and the dislodged front Tesla emblem — is documented in the photographs above and is consistent in location and mechanism with the impact shown on video.

VanCity Parking is responsible for the actions of its valet employee and for the damage to the vehicle entrusted to it.
